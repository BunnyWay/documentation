Once your hosting provider has installed and configured the bunny.net cPanel plugin, you can enable bunny.net for any of your websites directly from cPanel.

## Open the plugin

Log in to cPanel. In the sidebar, click **bunny.net** to open the plugin.

<Frame>
  ![bunny.net in cPanel sidebar](/images/integrations/cpanel/cpanel_home.png)
</Frame>

The plugin lists all websites on your account. Click **Configure** on the website you want to enable.

<Frame>
  ![bunny.net domain list](/images/integrations/cpanel/cpanel_plugin.png)
</Frame>

## Enable bunny.net

The setup wizard walks you through four steps automatically.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Checking website settings">
    The plugin analyzes your website's current settings and DNS configuration.

    <Frame>
      ![Checking website settings](/images/integrations/cpanel/enable_step1.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adding your website to Bunny DNS">
    Your website is added to Bunny DNS. DNS records are automatically synced between cPanel and Bunny DNS, so you can continue managing them through the cPanel Zone Editor.

    <Frame>
      ![Adding domain to Bunny DNS](/images/integrations/cpanel/enable_step2.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update nameservers">
    Update your nameservers at your registrar to point to bunny.net. The plugin displays the nameservers you need to set and instructions for your registrar.

    Click **Check Nameserver Configuration** to verify the change has propagated.

    <Frame>
      ![Update nameservers](/images/integrations/cpanel/enable_step3.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enabling Bunny CDN">
    Bunny CDN is activated for your website and an SSL certificate is provisioned automatically.

    <Frame>
      ![Enabling Bunny CDN](/images/integrations/cpanel/enable_step4.png)
    </Frame>
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once all four steps are complete, your website is live on bunny.net.

<Frame>
  ![Setup complete](/images/integrations/cpanel/enable_step5.png)
</Frame>
